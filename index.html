FAQ

Here are a few things we get frequently asked/ we'd like you to know.

Am I eligible for blood donation?

If you are 18-65 years old an weigh at least 50kg, it is very likely that you are eligible for the donation. Here are a few things that make you ineligible for blood donation:
  1. If you have consumed alcohol in the past 24 hours.
  2. If you were tested as HIV positive.
  3. If you have had fits, tuberculosis, asthma and allergic disorders in the past.
  4. If you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
  5. If you donated blood in the last 3 months.
Here is a more exhaustive list.

What are some of the myths related to blood donation?

Needles Hurt

Well, death of a loved one because they couldn't get timely blood hurts more.

The needle used for blood donations is the same size of the needle used when you draw blood for medical tests. It’s only a small prick. You can do it!

There is limited blood in the body and to give some away from the body is bad for health

Your body keeps on producing blood and replenishes blood quickly after you’ve donated. The body contains enough blood to enable you to donate without any serious consequences.

Taking medication means that one cannot be a blood donor.

Depending on the medication being taken, it may halt donation for a period, though in many cases it won't prevent a donation. person in charge or the nursing staff should be informed before donating.

Blood donation can tell if one is HIV positive.

HIV antibodies can take months to develop after infection with the virus. Those recently infected may have a negative test result and yet be able to infect others. It is better not to donate blood if at risk of getting HIV or other infections.

If you still have some doubt or concern, check if it is listed here or contact us .

There are plenty of donors. I don't need to donate blood.

According to WHO data, India faces a shortage of 3 million blood units every year. This shortage can easily be eliminated if only an additional 2% of India's youth donates blood. Also, there are several patients who require regular periodic blood transfusions to simply stay alive. With your contribution, more of such patients can live to see the light of another day. Be the hero we need

If I donate blood now, and one of my family members requires blood within the next three months, I won't be able to help then.

You will be given a donor card valid for at least three months which will put you on priority if you or a family member requires blood during the validity period of the card.

Who are you people? What is NSS-DTU ?

The National Service Scheme is an Indian government-sponsored public service program conducted by the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports of the Government of India. The NSS-DTU chapter was inaugurated in 2013 to establish a meaningful linkage between the campus and the community. The overall aim of NSS-DTU is to give an extended dimension to the higher education system and orient the students towards community service while they are studying in the institution.

We are a subset of NSS-DTU working on making the process of connecting blood donors and patients more efficient. In the later half of 2018 alone, we, the blood donation team of NSS-DTU, fulfilled 13 blood donation requests which were raised by DTU students or faculty members. Feel free to contact us in the hour of need.
